# freejobpost.co — Employer outreach templates

**Purpose:** Diversify the supply side. Currently 99.8% of jobs on the board are
Ava-seeded. Phase 1 plan target: ~85% seeded / ~15% real third-party employers
within 90 days, which means ~25 verified employer signups. These templates are
the cold-outbound vehicle for that.

**Cadence:** Every few hours, pick one recipient + one template variant, paste into
Gmail Compose (alex@avahealth.co), customize the first line with one specific
reference about that person/practice, send. ~10–15 sends/day is sustainable.
Don't burst.

**Why manual + per-recipient personalization:** Standard S2 (human-touch) and
S1 (cold-outreach standards). Anything that looks like a campaign — same body
across many sends, scraped emails, no specific opener — gets filtered or
flagged for sender reputation.

---

## How to use a template

1. **Pick a real recipient.** Someone whose role + email you can verify
   (LinkedIn → company website → "Contact" or "Careers" page). Don't guess
   `first.last@hospital.com` patterns. If the address isn't publicly listed,
   pass on that prospect.
2. **Pick the template variant** that matches the employer profile (small
   practice / multi-site / hospital).
3. **Pick a subject line** from the variant pool — vary across sends so a
   single batch doesn't look templated.
4. **Customize the first line** with one specific real reference (a recent
   posting you saw, a directory mention, a mutual contact, news about their
   expansion). This is the single highest-impact thing — without it, the
   email reads like a campaign and the rest doesn't matter.
5. **Replace the [CUSTOMIZE …] placeholder.** Send.

## Tracking sends

Optional, but the data compounds: keep a small log of who you sent to,
when, which template, and reply status. A Google Sheet works. Columns:
`date`, `recipient_email`, `recipient_name`, `employer`, `template`,
`subject_used`, `reply_status (none/no/maybe/yes)`, `notes`.

The 30-day cool-down rule from S1 applies even to manual sends — don't
re-send to anyone you contacted in the last 30 days.

## Realistic conversion expectations

- ~10% reply rate
- ~2% sign up + post a real first job within 30 days
- 50 sends ≈ 1 real verified third-party employer

Twenty-five verified employers over 90 days is the Phase 1 target. That
math says you need ~1,250 sends, or 14/day. Sustainable manually. The
templates should hold up across that volume because each send has its
own customized opener.

## When to refine

After ~25 sends per template:
- If reply rate is <5% on a variant, the subject + first line aren't
  landing — try a new opener pattern.
- If reply rate is >15%, lock that variant in and rotate it more.
- Take note of which industry/employer-size segment is replying — Phase 2
  decisions (sponsored tiers, ATS integration priority) hinge on knowing
  who's actually using the board.
